Shakespeare's Troilus and Cressida (A2/B1)

Created by Panagiotis Domvros, English and Drama Teacher

The story takes place in the seventh year of the Trojan War. The Greek army is outside the city of Troy, but the war is not going well. The Greek leaders, like Agamemnon and Ulysses, are worried because their best warrior, Achilles, is proud and refuses to fight.

Inside Troy, a young prince named Troilus is not interested in the war. He is deeply in love with a beautiful woman named Cressida. Cressida's uncle, Pandarus, promises to help Troilus by arranging a secret meeting between the two lovers.

After much planning by Pandarus, Troilus and Cressida finally meet. They confess their love for each other and promise to be faithful forever. They spend one night together, hoping their love will last even during the terrible war.

In the Greek camp, Achilles spends his days in his tent with his close friend, Patroclus. He makes fun of the Greek leaders and refuses to listen to their orders. His pride is so great that he believes the Greek army cannot win without him.

The clever Greek leader, Ulysses, comes up with a plan to hurt Achilles's pride. When the Trojan hero Hector challenges any Greek to a duel, Ulysses convinces the leaders to choose the strong but foolish warrior Ajax instead of Achilles. They hope this will make Achilles jealous and want to fight again.

Meanwhile, Cressida's father, Calchas, who is a Trojan priest living with the Greeks, makes a deal. He asks the Greek leaders to exchange a Trojan prisoner for his daughter, Cressida. The Greeks agree, and they send a soldier named Diomedes to bring Cressida from Troy to the Greek camp.

Troilus and Cressida are heartbroken. They have to say goodbye after only one night together. Troilus gives Cressida his sleeve as a token to remember him by, and Cressida promises she will be true to him and never love a Greek.

When Cressida arrives at the Greek camp, the leaders are all impressed by her beauty. She is now alone and in a dangerous place. The soldier Diomedes, who brought her to the camp, immediately starts to show interest in her.

The duel between Hector and Ajax takes place. Because they are cousins, they decide not to fight to the death. They fight for a short time and then stop. That night, the Trojan leaders, including Hector and Troilus, have a feast with the Greek leaders in the Greek camp.

During the feast, Troilus asks Ulysses to take him to see Cressida. They hide and watch as the Greek soldier, Diomedes, talks with Cressida. Troilus sees Cressida give Diomedes the sleeve he had given her as a love token. She agrees to be with Diomedes.

Troilus is completely devastated. His heart is broken by Cressida's betrayal. He decides that his love is dead and that from now on, he will only think about war and getting revenge on Diomedes.

The next day, the fighting is very fierce. Hector's wife, Andromache, and his sister, Cassandra, beg him not to fight. They have had bad dreams and believe he will die. But Hector says he must fight for his honor.

During the battle, Hector kills Patroclus, Achilles's best friend. When Achilles hears this news, his pride turns into a desire for revenge. He decides to enter the battle and find Hector.

Achilles and his soldiers, the Myrmidons, find Hector when he is resting and has taken off his armor. In a dishonorable act, Achilles orders his men to attack the unarmed Hector. They kill him without a fair fight.

Achilles shows no honor. He ties Hector's dead body to the back of his horse and drags it around the walls of Troy for everyone to see. The Trojans have lost their greatest hero in the worst way possible.

The play ends with no hope. Troilus is filled with sadness and anger over Cressida's betrayal and Hector's death. The war continues, and the story of the two lovers is lost in the violence and tragedy of the conflict.

Scenes and Dialogues

Picture 1: The Trojan War

Agamemnon: For seven years we have fought this war!

Nestor: The city of Troy is strong.

Ulysses: And our own hero, Achilles, will not fight.

Picture 2: Troilus in Love

Troilus: I cannot fight. My heart is wounded by love.

Pandarus: Be patient, my prince. I will speak to Cressida for you.

Troilus: My love for her is a war inside me!

Picture 3: The Secret Meeting

Pandarus: Now, speak to each other! I have done my part.

Troilus: Cressida, I love you more than words can say.

Cressida: And I have loved you for a long time, my lord.

Picture 4: Achilles's Pride

Achilles: Why should I fight for Agamemnon? He is a fool.

Patroclus: Let's stay in our tent and laugh at the other leaders.

Achilles: They are nothing without me.

Picture 5: Ulysses's Plan

Ulysses: We must make Achilles jealous.

Agamemnon: How?

Ulysses: Let Hector challenge a Greek. We will choose Ajax, not Achilles. His pride will be hurt!

Picture 6: The Exchange

Agamemnon: We will trade our prisoner for your daughter, Calchas.

Diomedes: I will go to Troy and bring Cressida here.

Calchas: Thank you, my lords.

Picture 7: The Sad Goodbye

Cressida: We only had one night together.

Troilus: Be true to me, Cressida. Do not forget me.

Cressida: I will be faithful. I promise.

Picture 8: Cressida's Arrival

Ulysses: Welcome to the Greek camp, Cressida.

Diomedes: She is very beautiful.

Cressida: I am a stranger here. I must be careful.

Picture 9: The Duel of Cousins

Hector: Ajax, you are my cousin. We should not fight to the death.

Ajax: You are right. Let us stop.

Hector: Tonight, we will feast together as friends!

Picture 10: Cressida's Betrayal

Diomedes: Give me that sleeve as a promise.

Cressida: It was a gift from a Trojan... but yes, you can have it.

Troilus: (hiding) She breaks her promise! It is not possible!

Picture 11: A Broken Heart

Troilus: My love is gone. My heart is broken.

Ulysses: This is what happens in war, young prince.

Troilus: Tomorrow, I will find Diomedes on the battlefield!

Picture 12: Hector's Last Day

Andromache: Do not fight today, Hector! I had a bad dream.

Cassandra: Listen to her! Today is a day of death!

Hector: I must fight. My honor is more important than my life.

Picture 13: The Death of Patroclus

Soldier: Achilles! Hector has killed your friend, Patroclus!

Achilles: Patroclus? No!

Achilles: Now I will fight! Hector will die for this!

Picture 14: The Dishonorable Kill

Achilles: I have found you, Hector! You are unarmed.

Hector: This is not an honorable way to fight, Achilles.

Achilles: Myrmidons, attack! Kill him now!

Picture 15: The Fall of a Hero

Achilles: Tie his body to my horse!

Achilles: Drag him through the dirt! Let all of Troy see their hero now!

Soldier: A terrible day.

Picture 16: No Hope

Troilus: Hector is dead. Cressida is false.

Troilus: There is no hope left. Only war.

Pandarus: This is the end of our story.
